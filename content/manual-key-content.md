## Über die Methode

### A Meeting Place of Structure and Feeling

In this training, we explore the space where body and psyche meet — where connective tissue, emotions, and awareness weave together.
Our work is not only about releasing tension, but about creating the conditions for the nervous system to soften, for old holding patterns to unwind, and for deeper layers of presence to emerge.

### Fascia as Living Memory

Fascia — the body's connective tissue web — is both structure and sensing organ. It surrounds and interlinks every muscle, bone, organ, and nerve, making it one continuous whole. More than scaffolding, fascia is alive and responsive: it adapts to stress, posture, and emotion.
When we brace against pain or suppress feeling, fascia thickens and contracts, holding those experiences in place. Over time, these imprints shape how we breathe, how we move, and even how we meet the world.

### Why We Work with Fascia

Touching fascia with clarity, patience, and listening hands awakens the body's intelligence. Release often comes as a softening of tension, a fuller breath, a new sense of support. Sometimes it stirs emotion or memory, as the body lets go of what it has carried silently.
This is why fascia is central to somatic bodywork: it holds both the physical and the emotional story of our lives.

### The Body as Landscape of Meaning

Every body part is both anatomical and symbolic. The back holds what is unseen; the chest opens us to breath and vulnerability; the arms extend connection; the legs ground us into the earth. By touching these areas with awareness, we don't just work with tissue — we meet the whole human story.

## Kernphilosophie — "When you meet something hard, become soft"

This simple sentence has become one of the most important truths I carry—
in my approach to deep emotional bodywork, and in how I meet life.

When I first started working with the body, I often found myself wanting to *do something* to the "hard stuff" I found in the tissue.
I'd press, knead, work through it with focus and force.
And while that sometimes brought change, it often came with a subtle tension—in my body and in theirs.

But over time, I began to notice something different.
When I slowed down... when I simply stayed with the hardness, let my hands rest and listen, let my body soften and feel— that's when everything started to change.

**Softness is a language.** And when I meet the body with softness, the body feels it. It responds. It relaxes. It begins to trust.

Softness is not weakness. It's contact. It's presence. It's choice.
In the body. In the session. In life.

## Schlüsselprinzipien der Arbeit mit Faszien

- **Patience** – Fascia responds to time, not force. Stay connected long enough for the tissue to feel safe to open.
- **Sinking step by step** – Rather than pushing through, soften in stages and follow the depth of the tissue.
- **Hands that listen** – Release comes when touch listens more than it imposes.
- **Dialogue, not technique** – Touch is a conversation with living tissue, an invitation for the body to remember its fluid, resilient nature.

## Emotionale Körperbereiche (aus Deutschen Skripten)

### Unterer Rücken / Kreuzbein (Sacrum)
Zu viel Druck (von außen oder häufig auch selbst auferlegt). Der untere Rücken ist nicht durch Rippen oder andere Knochenstrukturen geschützt, sondern nur durch starke Muskeln.

**Potenzial:** wenn wir uns unseren Kernthemen stellen und die Angst vor ständigen Angriffen aus der Vergangenheit lösen, kann der untere Rücken entspannen und Länge gewinnen.

### Pomuskulatur
"Schiss haben"; Unterdrückung der eigenen Bedürfnisse und Wünsche.

**Potenzial:** Mehr Geschmeidigkeit und Beweglichkeit im Becken führt zu signifikantem Stressabbau im gesamten System.

### Bauch & Iliopsoas
Der Psoas-Muskel als Verbindung zwischen Unter- und Oberkörper. Intensivere Kommunikation zwischen den drei Hauptzentren: Herz (Feuer) – Kopf (Luft/Himmel) – Becken/Beine (Wasser/Erde).
